## 1. Review Purpose

This review evaluates `PROGRAM-0001 v0.2 Draft` for architectural coherence, governance completeness, normative clarity, publication readiness, and consistency with the foundational direction of the AISET Program.

This is an internal foundation-stage review.

It is not represented as an independent external review.

---

## 2. Review Scope

The review covers:

- program identity and mission;
- scope and boundaries;
- foundational architectural principles;
- document hierarchy;
- program roles and authorities;
- review and decision procedures;
- approval and publication authority;
- versioning and immutability;
- registry requirements;
- normative and informative material;
- conformance;
- licensing;
- security and privacy;
- amendments;
- continuity and succession.

The review does not constitute:

- legal advice;
- an external security audit;
- an independent standards-body review;
- implementation conformance testing;
- final publisher approval.

---

## 3. Review Criteria

The document was evaluated against the following criteria:

1. architectural consistency;
2. technology independence;
3. terminology coherence;
4. separation of normative architecture and implementation;
5. separation of program roles and authorities;
6. traceability of approval and publication decisions;
7. version and release immutability;
8. governance continuity;
9. compatibility with future delegated governance;
10. preservation of authorship, provenance, and program origin.

---

## 4. Findings Summary

The review identified five principal areas requiring strengthening in the earlier `v0.1 Draft`:

1. normative status of the Charter;
2. hierarchy and precedence of AISET documents;
3. separation of program roles and authorities;
4. review, disagreement, conflict-of-interest, and decision procedures;
5. classification of major, minor, patch, and pre-release versions.

These areas were addressed in `v0.2 Draft`.

---

## 5. Normative Status Review

### Finding

The earlier draft did not sufficiently distinguish between:

- binding program-level requirements;
- descriptive background;
- implementation guidance;
- draft provisions not yet in force.

### Resolution

`v0.2 Draft` now:

- identifies the Charter as an intended program-level normative document;
- identifies the provisions intended to become normative;
- distinguishes informative material from normative material;
- introduces explicit normative terms;
- states that the draft remains non-binding until approval.

### Outcome

**Resolved.**

---

## 6. Document Hierarchy Review

### Finding

The earlier draft described repository areas but did not establish formal precedence among Charter, governance, architecture, specifications, schemas, profiles, and implementations.

### Resolution

`v0.2 Draft` now defines a document hierarchy and states that:

- lower-level documents must not override higher-level documents;
- informative material must not redefine normative requirements;
- conflicts must be recorded and resolved through governed mechanisms;
- higher-level approved documents remain authoritative pending resolution.

### Outcome

**Resolved.**

---

## 7. Program Authority Review

### Finding

The earlier draft defined roles but did not fully distinguish:

- authorship authority;
- editorial authority;
- review authority;
- approval authority;
- publication authority;
- registry authority;
- repository maintenance authority.

### Resolution

`v0.2 Draft` now defines these authorities separately and clarifies that:

- one person may temporarily perform multiple roles;
- each action must remain attributable to the applicable role;
- operational delegation does not transfer historical authorship or program origination;
- future governance may delegate authority without erasing provenance.

### Outcome

**Resolved.**

---

## 8. Review and Decision Process Review

### Finding

The earlier draft did not define sufficient procedures for:

- review outcomes;
- blocking findings;
- conflicts of interest;
- reviewer disagreement;
- decisions without consensus;
- appeals;
- urgent corrections;
- publication suspension or withdrawal.

### Resolution

`v0.2 Draft` now defines:

- formal review outcomes;
- requirements for blocking findings;
- conflict-of-interest disclosure;
- handling of unresolved disagreement;
- decision records;
- appeal conditions;
- urgent correction procedures;
- suspension and withdrawal conditions.

### Outcome

**Resolved.**

---

## 9. Versioning Review

### Finding

The earlier draft required explicit versions and immutable releases but did not distinguish the normative effects of major, minor, patch, and pre-release changes.

### Resolution

`v0.2 Draft` now defines:

- major version changes;
- minor version changes;
- patch version changes;
- pre-release identifiers;
- immutable release requirements;
- classification according to normative effect rather than textual size.

### Outcome

**Resolved.**

---

## 10. Architectural Consistency

The reviewed Charter remains consistent with the following foundational AISET positions:

- AISET standardizes cognitive context transformations rather than intelligence;
- normative architecture remains technology-independent;
- Cognitive Context Objects are processed through Cognitive Transformations;
- implementations remain replaceable and non-normative unless explicitly specified;
- compatibility and interoperability are evaluated against identified specifications;
- shared cognitive evolution requires traceable and governed context change;
- trust, provenance, measurement, and versioning are transversal concerns.

### Outcome

**No blocking inconsistency identified.**

---

## 11. Governance Consistency

The reviewed Charter provides a sufficient foundation for later governance documents concerning:

- delegated approval authority;
- working groups;
- review bodies;
- publication councils;
- registry administration;
- appeals;
- succession;
- amendments;
- conformance governance.

The Charter does not attempt to prematurely define all future governance structures.

### Outcome

**Acceptable for the foundation stage.**

---

## 12. Open Non-Blocking Items

The following matters remain suitable for future documents or later Charter revisions:

- formal definitions of normative terms;
- detailed publication class requirements;
- review templates;
- approval record schemas;
- registry record schemas;
- governance council composition;
- quorum and voting rules;
- formal appeals body;
- succession activation procedures;
- machine-readable conformance reporting;
- security review methodology.

These items do not block progression of `PROGRAM-0001 v0.2 Draft` to formal Review status.
