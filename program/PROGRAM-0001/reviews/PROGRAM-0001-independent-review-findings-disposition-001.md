## 4. INDEPENDENT-001 Disposition

**Disposition:** Resolved

The Program accepts the finding.

The original Charter permitted a theoretical pathway in which a higher-level governance rule, foundation-stage exception, or delegation could be created or used by the same person whose authority would be expanded or validated.

PROGRAM-0001 v1.0 Review was amended in Sections `12.11`, `13.3`, `13.4`, and `31.5`.

The amended Charter now requires that:

- a foundation-stage exception must not substitute for independent final approval;
- a higher-level governance rule affecting approval or exception authority must not be created or activated solely by the person benefiting from it;
- an exception affecting final approval or authority separation must receive documented independent or second-party confirmation;
- delegation affecting review or approval authority must not be made to the delegating person under another role title;
- delegation must not be made to an affiliate or body materially controlled by the delegating person;
- delegation must not convert a non-independent action into an apparently independent confirmation;
- revocation, expiration, reassignment, succession, or replacement must not validate an action that was non-independent or self-approved when performed;
- a non-circular exception mechanism must exist before an exception is proposed;
- the beneficiary of an exception must not create or approve the mechanism used to authorize that exception;
- an exception must not authorize self-approval, approval under another title, controlled-affiliate approval, or retroactive validation.

No material residual self-approval pathway remains within the identified scope.

**Final Status:** `INDEPENDENT-001 — Resolved`

## 5. INDEPENDENT-002 Disposition

**Disposition:** Resolved

The Program accepts the finding.

PROGRAM-0001 v1.0 Review was amended in Section `13.2`.

The amended Charter now requires that:

- the Approval Authority established before first final approval must be distinct from the Program Originator;
- foundation-stage exception authority expires automatically upon the earlier of:
  - the first Approved Publication of PROGRAM-0001; or
  - establishment of an Approval Authority distinct from the Program Originator;
- after that trigger, foundation-stage role overlap, delegation, exception, registry action, or Publisher acknowledgment may not satisfy a requirement for independent approval.

The Charter now contains an explicit and automatic transition trigger.

**Final Status:** `INDEPENDENT-002 — Resolved`
