## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this package is to obtain a documented second-party assessment of whether PROGRAM-0001 is ready to progress from Review toward Candidate Publication.

The requested reviewer is not asked to rewrite the Charter.

The reviewer is asked to identify:

- blocking issues;
- major issues;
- minor issues;
- unclear normative boundaries;
- inaccurate or ambiguous cross-references;
- governance conflicts;
- independence concerns;
- Candidate Publication readiness concerns.

## 2. Review Independence

The requested reviewer must act independently from:

- the Program Originator;

- the Lead Editor;

The reviewer must disclose any actual, potential, or perceived conflict of interest.

The reviewer may decline or recuse if independence cannot be maintained.

## 6. Review Scope

The review scope includes:

- governance coherence;
- role separation;
- appeal and reconsideration provisions;
- approval authority safeguards;
- conflict-of-interest handling;
- normative and informative boundaries;
- cross-reference precision;
- lifecycle-state consistency;
- security and privacy safeguards;
- integrity and provenance requirements;
- operational record sufficiency;
- Candidate Publication readiness.

The review does not require:

- legal advice;
- security certification;
- privacy certification;
- technical implementation validation;
- external standards accreditation.

---

## 7. Required Review Questions

The reviewer should answer each question.

### 7.1 Governance

1. Are authority roles sufficiently separated?
2. Are conflicts of interest handled adequately?
3. Are appeal and reconsideration mechanisms understandable and usable?
4. Are any authority relationships circular or ambiguous?
5. Are any powers broader than necessary?

### 7.2 Normative Boundaries

6. Is it clear which provisions are normative?
7. Is it clear which examples, explanations, and notes are informative?
8. Are any informative statements written as if they were mandatory?
9. Are any mandatory rules weakened by ambiguous wording?
10. Does ED-002 remain materially open?

### 7.3 Cross-references

11. Do the reviewed cross-references point to the intended sections or records?
12. Are document identifiers and version relationships consistent?
13. Are registry and Publisher relationships accurately described?
14. Are any references missing, circular, or misleading?
15. Does ED-003 remain materially open?

### 7.4 Security and Privacy

16. Are security-sensitive decisions adequately controlled?
17. Are personal data and restricted records handled appropriately?
18. Are incident, recovery, correction, and integrity requirements sufficient?
19. Are any critical safeguards missing?

### 7.5 Lifecycle Readiness

20. Is PROGRAM-0001 v1.0 Review suitable to proceed toward Candidate Publication?
21. Are there any blocking findings?
22. Are there any major findings requiring revision before Candidate Publication?
23. May NEW-005 be considered resolved by this completed review?
24. What final outcome does the reviewer recommend?

---

## 8. Finding Severity

The reviewer should classify each finding as:

- **Blocking** — Candidate Publication must not proceed;
- **Major** — material revision required before Candidate Publication;
- **Minor** — correction recommended but not lifecycle-blocking;
- **Observation** — non-blocking comment or future enhancement;
- **No Finding** — no issue identified.

Each finding should identify:

- finding identifier;
- severity;
- affected section or record;
- issue;
- reason;
- recommended action;
- disposition recommendation.

## 12. NEW-005 Closure Test

NEW-005 may be recorded as resolved only when:

- the reviewer is a real second person;
- independence and conflicts are disclosed;
- the Charter or Candidate-readiness materials were actually reviewed;
- findings are documented;
- an outcome is issued;
- the review record may be preserved;
- the reviewer is not falsely represented as providing certification.

Appointment alone does not satisfy this test.

---

## 13. ED-002 Review Test

ED-002 may be recorded as resolved when the reviewer confirms that:

- normative requirements are distinguishable from informative content;
- examples and explanatory text do not create hidden obligations;
- mandatory language is used consistently;
- no material ambiguity remains.

If ambiguity remains, the reviewer should identify exact sections.

---

## 14. ED-003 Review Test

ED-003 may be recorded as resolved when the reviewer confirms that:

- cross-references are accurate;
- referenced identifiers and versions are consistent;
- lifecycle relationships are understandable;
- no material missing or misleading reference remains.

If an error remains, the reviewer should identify the exact reference.

---

## 15. Candidate Publication Readiness Test

The reviewer should consider Candidate Publication readiness only as a recommendation.

Final Candidate Publication requires separate:

- readiness review;
- integrity record;
- checksum verification;
- approval decision;
- registry record;
- Publisher acknowledgment.

The reviewer does not create those records through this response.
