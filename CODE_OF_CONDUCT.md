# AISET Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The AISET Program is committed to maintaining a professional, respectful, inclusive, and technically constructive environment for all participants.

This Code of Conduct applies to contributors, reviewers, editors, maintainers, implementers, researchers, users, and other participants in AISET-related spaces.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully and professionally;
- evaluate ideas on their technical, architectural, and evidential merits;
- provide constructive and specific feedback;
- acknowledge uncertainty and correct mistakes;
- respect differences in experience, background, perspective, and technical approach;
- preserve clear authorship, attribution, and provenance;
- avoid unnecessary personal conflict;
- respect privacy and confidential information;
- follow applicable laws, licenses, and repository policies;
- support a safe and productive environment for collaboration.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- harassment, intimidation, or threats;
- personal attacks or insulting comments;
- discriminatory language or conduct;
- deliberate disruption of discussions or reviews;
- repeated bad-faith argumentation;
- impersonation or misrepresentation;
- unauthorized disclosure of private or confidential information;
- plagiarism or false authorship claims;
- manipulation of review, approval, provenance, or publication records;
- knowingly submitting malicious code, deceptive artifacts, or falsified test results;
- retaliation against a person who reports a concern in good faith.

## Technical and Architectural Discussion

Disagreement is expected in engineering and standards work.

Participants should:

- distinguish criticism of an idea from criticism of a person;
- identify assumptions and supporting evidence;
- describe compatibility, interoperability, security, privacy, and governance implications;
- avoid presenting implementation preferences as normative requirements without justification;
- document unresolved disagreements where they materially affect a publication or specification.

## Review Conduct

Reviewers should:

- review the submitted material rather than the contributor personally;
- provide actionable findings;
- distinguish blocking issues from recommendations;
- disclose relevant conflicts of interest;
- avoid using review authority to suppress legitimate alternatives;
- preserve traceability of significant review decisions.

Contributors should:

- respond to review findings professionally;
- ask for clarification where needed;
- avoid concealing known limitations;
- document accepted deviations where applicable.

## Authorship and Attribution

Participants must not:

- claim authorship of work they did not create;
- remove valid attribution without justification;
- falsify contribution, review, approval, or publication records;
- use automated assistance to misrepresent human review or authorship.

Use of tools, including artificial intelligence systems, should not obscure responsibility for submitted material.

The person submitting or approving a contribution remains responsible for its accuracy, provenance, licensing, and compliance.

## Security and Responsible Disclosure

Potential security vulnerabilities should not be publicly exploited or disclosed in a way that creates unnecessary risk.

A participant who discovers a security issue should report it responsibly through the available project contact channel.

A dedicated security policy and reporting process may be introduced as AISET implementations develop.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within:

- repository issues and discussions;
- pull requests and code reviews;
- publication and specification reviews;
- AISET communication channels;
- public events and meetings;
- private communications made in connection with AISET participation;
- other spaces where a person is representing the AISET Program.

## Reporting

Concerns should be reported with as much relevant information as practical, including:

- a description of the conduct;
- dates and locations;
- links or records;
- persons involved;
- any immediate safety or security concerns.

Reports should be handled with appropriate discretion.

A formal reporting address and enforcement process will be established as the contributor community develops.

## Enforcement

Responses may include:

1. clarification or informal guidance;
2. a request to correct behavior;
3. a formal warning;
4. removal of content;
5. temporary participation restrictions;
6. permanent exclusion from AISET-managed spaces;
7. referral to a platform provider or relevant authority where necessary.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, documented, and based on available evidence.

## Good-Faith Reporting

Retaliation against a person who reports a concern in good faith is prohibited.

Knowingly false or malicious reports may themselves constitute a violation.

## Current Status

The AISET Program is currently in its initial foundation stage.

This document establishes the baseline conduct expectations for current and future AISET collaboration.

More detailed reporting, moderation, appeal, and enforcement procedures may be introduced as the program develops.
